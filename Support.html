Understanding Your Readings

Heart Beat Reading(BPM)

Understanding your heart rate is crucial for monitoring your cardiovascular health. Here's what your readings mean:

  • Green (Normal): 60–100 BPM - Healthy, keep up with your routine!
  • Yellow (Caution): 50–59 BPM or 101–110 BPM - Monitor closely, consider consulting a doctor if persistent.
  • Red (Warning): <50 BPM or >110 BPM - Immediate medical attention recommended.

Tip: Regular exercise, stress management, and hydration can help maintain a healthy heart rate.

SpO2 Reading

Your blood oxygen level indicates how well oxygen is distributed throughout your body. Pay attention to these levels:

Healthy Range: 95–100% healthy condition

Hypoxemia: Levels below 80–85% can lead to cognitive and visual impairments.

Severe Risk: At 67%, the risk of cyanosis increases, causing blue discoloration of nails, skin, and mucous membranes.

If you experience breathing difficulties, confusion, or blue-tinted skin, seek medical attention immediately.

Tip: Practice breathing exercises, avoid smoking, and ensure adequate air quality to maintain optimal oxygen levels.
